Reasons to move towards Thorium

  1. Energy Density
    • 26 grams of thorium is = to 17-27 barrels of crude oil.
    • In order to match your average thorium nuclear plant with "renewable" energy you would need 42 of General Electric's newest wind turbines, the GE Haliade X (the biggest turbine ever made) costing $89 million and $420 million respectively.
  2. Polution
    • Does not burn fuel, so there are no particles released into the air.
    • Creates radioactive waste rather than chemical, so it is only waste for a set amount of time.
    • Creates a small fraction of the nuclear waste that Uranium decay chains do, and that amount lasts a fraction of that time.
  3. Availability
    • 26 grams of thorium is how much thorium you can find in your average 1 cubed meter of soil
    • In coal mining, vast amounts of thorium have already been brought the the surface and thrown away as a mining waste because we currently don't use it for anything.
